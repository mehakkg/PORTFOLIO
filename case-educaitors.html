AI-native Learning Platform

Designing an EdTech product from 0 to 1, where AI isn't a feature bolted on, but the foundation that makes adaptive, personalised learning actually possible.

The brief

EducAItors wanted to build a learning platform where AI does more than surface content: it adapts curriculum in real time based on how each learner thinks, not just what they answer. The design challenge: make a powerful AI engine feel intuitive, trustworthy, and human.

Defining the surfaces

Three distinct users with completely different goals, mental models, and usage contexts. I started by mapping what success looked like for each.

Surface 01

Learner experience

Students needed to feel the platform was learning them, not just testing them. The AI's decisions had to feel natural, not like a black box generating random exercises.

"I want to feel like the platform gets me, not that it's just throwing stuff at me."
Surface 02

Educator dashboard

Teachers needed visibility into AI decisions without being overwhelmed. They needed to trust, override, and learn from the system, not compete with it.

"I need to know why the AI thinks this student is struggling before I intervene."
Surface 03

Institution admin panel

Admins cared about cohort-level data, compliance, and curriculum governance. A completely different information density and interaction model from the learner view.

"Show me where outcomes are falling short across all classes, not individual students."
Cross-cutting concern

Explainable AI

Every AI recommendation needed a plain-language explanation. "Why is the platform showing me this?" had to be answerable in one tap, for all three user types.

"If I don't understand it, I don't trust it. And if I don't trust it, I won't use it."

Design decisions

01
Adaptive learning path UI
A visual curriculum map that updates in real time. Learners see their path, branching points, and why upcoming content was chosen, demystifying the AI entirely.
02
"Why this?" explainability layer
A persistent tap-to-explain interaction available on every AI-driven piece of content. One layer deep for learners, two for educators, three for admins.
03
Educator override system
Teachers can override any AI recommendation with one click, add a reason (saved for model feedback), and see the impact on predicted outcomes before confirming.
04
Cohort insight dashboard
Institution admins get a heatmap view of curriculum performance, flagging topics where the AI's recommendations consistently diverge from educator overrides.
05
Unified design system
One component library serving all three surfaces. Same tokens, adapted densities, so the platform feels coherent even when the information architecture is completely different.
