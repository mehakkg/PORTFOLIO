SaltPeppr: Three Surfaces

Designing a unified product system for a consumer food platform: web, iOS, Android, and an ops display, as a solo designer in a single engagement.

Client
SaltPeppr
Year
2024
Role
Solo Designer
Domain
Consumer Food · Multi-platform

The brief

SaltPeppr needed a complete product design system covering four distinct surfaces: a consumer web app, iOS and Android native apps, and a kitchen operations display for restaurant staff. One designer, one system, shipped consistently across all four.

4
Distinct surfaces designed under a single system
1
Designer responsible for all surfaces end to end
3
User types: consumers, restaurant staff, and kitchen operators
1
Design system powering all surfaces from a single token set

The challenge of one system, many contexts

Surface 01

Consumer web & mobile

Browsing, ordering, and tracking, on a small screen, in a hurry. Information hierarchy needed to work at thumb reach with one hand, in bright outdoor light.

"I'm ordering food from the back of a cab. Make it work in 30 seconds."
Surface 02

Kitchen ops display

Restaurant staff read the display from 3 metres while cooking. No touch interaction. Information density had to be high, legibility higher.

"I have a pan in each hand. I need to see the next order without going near the screen."
Cross-surface tension

One brand, radically different needs

The same visual identity needed to work in a playful consumer context and a high-stress professional kitchen environment without feeling like two different products.

"It should feel like SaltPeppr whether you're ordering or cooking."
Constraint

Solo designer, full scope

No design team to divide the work. Every component, every screen, every prototype had to come from one person maintaining coherence across four surfaces.

The answer was systematic thinking before any screen design began.

Design approach

01
Token-first design system
Built the design system from tokens before any component: colour, spacing, type scale, and elevation defined once, applied everywhere with surface-specific overrides.
02
Consumer ordering flow
Streamlined to 4 taps from menu to confirmed order. Cart always accessible, address pre-filled, payment remembered, no friction on the critical path.
03
Native mobile patterns
iOS and Android built with platform-native interaction patterns, not a web view in a wrapper. Swipe gestures, haptic feedback, and OS-native navigation preserved.
04
Kitchen display system
Designed for distance reading: 48px minimum type, high-contrast status colours, order queue with time-remaining indicators. Auto-advancing, no touch required.
