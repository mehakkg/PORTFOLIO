Ambulance Dispatch Decision Support

Designing a real-time emergency dispatch system that helps coordinators make faster, safer decisions when every second counts.

Merry Health

The problem

Emergency ambulance coordinators were making life-critical dispatch decisions using fragmented tools — phone calls, spreadsheets, and a legacy CAD system that hadn't been updated in a decade. The result: delayed responses, misrouted vehicles, and coordinators burning out under cognitive load.

Research & constraints

This was a high-stakes, high-constraint domain. I embedded with coordinators across two shifts to understand their actual decision-making process.

Constraint 01

Cognitive load at peak

During a mass casualty event, coordinators manage 8–12 simultaneous incidents. Any interface that requires reading slows them down.

"I can't read anything during a surge. I go by colour and position."
Constraint 02

No room for ambiguity

A wrong dispatch decision has direct patient impact. The interface needed to surface the single best option without hiding alternatives.

"The system should tell me what it thinks, but I need to own the decision."
Constraint 03

Trust must be earned

Coordinators are experienced professionals. A system that hides its reasoning or overrides their judgment will be ignored within days.

"If I don't understand why it's suggesting that unit, I'll override it every time."
Constraint 04

Hardware limitations

The dispatch centre runs on aging monitors with low refresh rates. Animation-heavy UIs lag. The design had to work at 30fps on a 1080p screen.

"Our computers are slow. If it looks fancy it probably won't run."

Design approach

01
Incident triage view
A single-screen command view showing all active incidents ranked by acuity. Colour, icon, and position encode severity — no reading required at a glance.
02
Transparent recommendation engine
System surfaces the top 3 available units for each incident with ETA, capacity, and proximity shown — reasoning visible, decision owned by coordinator.
03
One-action dispatch
Confirming a dispatch is a single tap with an undo window. Removed all confirmation dialogs from the critical path.
04
Surge mode
A one-click mode that simplifies the interface to essential controls only during mass casualty events — hiding non-critical information automatically.
