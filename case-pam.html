PAM Platform Redesign

Rebuilding the security command centre for enterprise IT teams — from audit chaos to a system that actually prevents breaches.

Client
miniOrange
Year
2023 — 2026
Role
Sr. Product Designer
Domain
Privileged Access Management

The problem

Enterprise IT teams managing privileged accounts faced an overwhelming, fragmented interface. Password vaults, session recordings, approval workflows, and audit logs lived in disconnected corners of the product — causing compliance failures and security gaps.

Discovery & audit

Before touching Figma, I ran a scenario-driven audit across 12 enterprise customer sessions and 3 internal team interviews.

Problem 01

No single source of truth

Privileged accounts were managed across 4 separate views with no consolidated dashboard. Admins had to cross-reference tabs to complete basic tasks.

"I have 3 monitors and I still can't see everything I need at once."
Problem 02

Approval workflows buried

Just-in-time access requests required 7 clicks to approve. In emergencies, IT admins were bypassing the system entirely.

"When the server is down, I don't have time for six confirmation dialogs."
Problem 03

Audit logs unusable

Session recordings and audit logs were stored in raw table format with no filtering, tagging, or anomaly surfacing.

"I export to Excel and search there. The product search is useless."
Problem 04

Onboarding had no guardrails

New enterprise customers took 4+ days to configure their first privileged access policy. No guided setup, no defaults, no progressive disclosure.

"We scheduled a 2-day call with the customer success team just to get started."

Design approach

Strategy before screens. The architecture was decided before any wireframe — then each component was built to make the right action the fastest action.

01
Information architecture
Collapsed 4 navigation areas into a unified command structure. Privileged accounts, sessions, policies, and audit all visible from one sidebar.
02
Just-in-time access flow
Rebuilt the approval workflow from 7 steps to 2. Inline context, one-click approve/deny, and a persistent notifications tray for pending requests.
03
Intelligent audit surface
Replaced raw tables with a filterable, taggable session timeline. Anomaly flags surface automatically. Export to CSV preserved for compliance teams.
04
Guided onboarding system
Built a progressive setup wizard with opinionated defaults for common enterprise configurations. Admins reach first policy in under 30 minutes.
05
Design system foundation
Documented the first component library for the PAM product: tokens, patterns, and usage rules. The next designer can ship faster because this exists.
